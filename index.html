About

I’m Abdur Rahman, a cloud and DevOps enthusiast at St. Joseph’s Group of Institutions. My journey began with Linux and hands-on cloud fundamentals, growing through certifications like Cisco Introduction to Cybersecurity, AWS Academy Graduate (Semesters 1 & 2), AWS Cloud Clubs Infrastructure Camper, and Microsoft Learn modules on Azure Fundamentals. With guidance from mentors such as Dr. Karthi M, Aravinnth S, and Dharshana Priya V, I’ve actively participated in the 30-day Cloud Challenge, Qiskit FallFest 2024, TechXConf 2024, and Google Cloud Community India Chennai 2024.

I design secure, scalable, and automated cloud-native systems on AWS and Azure, focusing on CI/CD, infrastructure as code, containerization, monitoring, and practical Linux-first workflows. I enjoy turning ideas into reliable services—prioritizing simplicity, cost-awareness, and maintainability.

Currently, I’m building decentralized and cloud-integrated projects (Web3 wallet, NFT marketplace) and strengthening DevOps depth. I’m pursuing AWS Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02) and preparing for Microsoft Azure developer pathways. I love collaborating on real-world problems in cloud infrastructure, security, and DevOps.
